Knitter's Glossary

Knitting Stitches & Techniques

  • Bind Off (BO) – Finishing edge that secures live stitches.
  • Cable – Stitches crossed over one another to create a twisted pattern.
  • Cast On (CO) – Method of creating the first row of stitches on the needle.
  • Decrease (dec) – Technique to reduce the number of stitches.
  • Garter Stitch – Fabric made by knitting every row.
  • Increase (inc) – Technique to add stitches.
  • Knit Stitch – Basic stitch formed by pulling yarn through the front of a loop.
  • Purl Stitch – Basic stitch formed by pulling yarn through the back of a loop.
  • Ribbing – Alternating knit and purl stitches, often used for cuffs and hems.
  • Seed Stitch – Textured pattern alternating knit and purl stitches.
  • Stockinette Stitch – Knit on right side, purl on wrong side; smooth front, bumpy back.
  • Yarn Over (YO) – Increase created by wrapping yarn over the needle.

Common Knitting Abbreviations

  • BO – Bind off
  • CO – Cast on
  • dec – Decrease
  • inc – Increase
  • k – Knit
  • k2tog – Knit two stitches together
  • p – Purl
  • p2tog – Purl two stitches together
  • rep – Repeat
  • RS / WS – Right side / Wrong side
  • sl – Slip stitch
  • st(s) – Stitch(es)
  • tbl – Through back loop
  • yo – Yarn over

Types of Yarn & Fiber

  • Acrylic – Synthetic fiber; durable and inexpensive.
  • Alpaca – Soft, warm natural fiber with little elasticity.
  • Blend – Yarn made from two or more fibers.
  • Cashmere – Luxurious, soft fiber from cashmere goats.
  • Cotton – Plant-based fiber; breathable and inelastic.
  • Linen – Strong plant fiber; cool and crisp texture.
  • Merino Wool – Soft, fine wool commonly used in garments.
  • Mohair – Fuzzy fiber from angora goats.
  • Silk – Smooth, shiny natural fiber with drape.
  • Wool – Elastic, warm fiber from sheep.

Yarn Weights

  • Fingering – Thin yarn for lightweight garments and socks.
  • Sport – Light yarn for fine garments.
  • DK (Double Knitting) – Medium-light yarn.
  • Worsted – Medium-weight, versatile yarn.
  • Bulky – Thick yarn for quick projects.
  • Super Bulky – Very thick yarn for chunky items.

Clothing Construction Types

  • Bottom-Up – Garment knit from hem to shoulders.
  • Top-Down – Garment knit from neckline downward.
  • Flat Construction – Knit in rows and seamed together.
  • In-the-Round – Knit continuously using circular or double-pointed needles.
  • Raglan – Sleeve construction with diagonal seams.
  • Set-In Sleeve – Sleeve sewn into armhole for tailored fit.
  • Drop Shoulder – Sleeve attached below shoulder line.
  • Seamless – Garment knit without sewing pieces together.
  • Panel Construction – Individual sections knit separately and assembled.

Tools & Materials

  • Circular Needles – Two needle tips connected by a cable.
  • Double-Pointed Needles (DPNs) – Short needles for small circumferences.
  • Gauge – Measurement of stitches and rows per inch.
  • Row Counter – Tool for tracking rows.
  • Stitch Marker – Marker to indicate pattern sections.
  • Tapestry Needle – Blunt needle for finishing and weaving in ends.
